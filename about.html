Why This Exists

Some stories get one news cycle. This one doesn't end there.

The Problem

In Pakistan, cases involving journalists, activists, whistleblowers, and ordinary citizens who spoke up have a pattern: intense coverage for a week, a press conference, a promise of an inquiry — and then nothing. The news moves on. The file, quietly, does not stay open in the public's mind.

Names get remembered. Details don't. And details are exactly what accountability depends on.

What We Do Instead

Casebook Pakistan treats every case the way an investigator would: a timeline, a location, a status, the questions that were never answered, and the sources a reader can go verify for themselves.

Nothing here is dramatized. Nothing here is invented. If a detail can't be sourced, it's marked as unknown rather than filled in for effect.

What We Stand On

Four things this archive will not compromise on.

T

Truth Over Traffic

No headline is worded to shock. If the facts are quiet, the write-up stays quiet.

M

Memory Over Momentum

A case stays listed long after the news cycle forgets it existed.

S

Sourcing Over Speculation

Every claim links back to where it came from. No anonymous "some say."

D

Dignity Over Detail

Victims are named as people first — not reduced to the worst day of their life.

How A Case Gets Filed

Four steps, in this order, every time.

01

Reported

A case is flagged through public reporting or a community submission.

02

Cross-Checked

Details are checked against multiple independent news sources.

03

Documented

A timeline, status, and open questions are written up in the case format.

04

Published & Revisited

The case goes live — and gets updated the moment anything changes.

A Note From The Founder

I started Casebook Pakistan because I kept noticing the same thing: a case would dominate every timeline for a week, and then quietly disappear from public memory—not because it was resolved, but because nobody was still keeping score.

This isn't investigative journalism, and it isn't activism. It's something smaller and more stubborn: a running record, built case by case, that refuses to let "unresolved" quietly turn into "forgotten."

Every file here represents a real person, a real event, and questions that deserve to remain part of the public record. My goal is simple: to build an archive that values truth, memory, and documentation over noise and headlines.

Zainab Imtiaz Founder • Casebook Pakistan

How We Handle This Responsibly

Every case on this site is compiled from publicly available news reports and named sources — never rumor, and never unverified social media claims. Where facts are disputed, we say so instead of picking a side.

This archive is built for documentation and public memory, not for entertainment. If you are a family member of someone documented here and want a correction, addition, or removal request reviewed, please reach out through the Report a Case page.
